Can Urban Farming Feed Africa's Cities?

Published: June 2024 Topics: Urban Agriculture, Food Security

As Africa's urban population continues to grow—projected to reach 1.5 billion by 2030—the challenge of food security in cities has become increasingly pressing. Traditional agricultural practices often struggle to keep pace with urbanization, leading to food deserts and rising prices. However, urban farming is emerging as a viable solution to this complex problem.

Urban farming refers to the practice of cultivating, processing, and distributing food in or around urban areas. This can take many forms, including community gardens, rooftop farms, and vertical farming systems. In cities like Nairobi, Kenya, and Accra, Ghana, urban agriculture is not just a trend; it is a necessity.
